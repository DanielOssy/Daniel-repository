Operational Scheduling Efficiency Review

Case No.RA–01
Prepared for                  
Period CoveredQ3 2023
AuthorD. Ebhohimen, Research & Operations
Illustrative Work Sample — Not a Verbatim Employer Document

1. Purpose

This review was commissioned to identify recurring scheduling conflicts affecting client-facing coordination teams and to recommend adjustments that reduce idle time without increasing headcount.

2. Method

Six weeks of shift-assignment logs, meeting-booking records, and stakeholder query timestamps were compiled into a single tracking sheet. Entries were tagged by department, resolution time, and whether a rescheduling event occurred. Patterns were checked against team size and reported workload to rule out coincidence before being treated as findings.

3. Findings

  • Rescheduling events clustered heavily in the first two hours after shift handover, suggesting a handover-documentation gap rather than a staffing shortfall.
  • Query resolution time was roughly 40% longer for cases requiring input from more than one department, driven by delays in routing rather than delays in the work itself.
  • No meaningful correlation was found between team size and conflict frequency — ruling out the "we just need more people" explanation that had been the working assumption.

4. Recommendation

Introduce a standard handover note format completed at shift end, and a single routing point for cross-department queries rather than ad hoc forwarding. Both are process changes, not staffing changes, and were proposed on that basis.

5. Outcome

Recommendations were adopted on a trial basis for one coordination team before wider rollout, with resolution-time tracking continued to confirm the effect held beyond the initial review window.
